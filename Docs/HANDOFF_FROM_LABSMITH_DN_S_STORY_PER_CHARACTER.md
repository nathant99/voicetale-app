## Cast OUTLINES

### Slow
**Primitive**: PACING across the 5-beat arc — the deliberate variation of tempo (slow / faster / sharp / slow / slow) across hook → setup → rising → turn → close.
**Premise**: Tortoise-elder with *wooden hourglass.* Her *tempo-trail* (visible behind her as she walks) *stretches* when she moves slowly and *bunches* when she moves fast. She demonstrates pacing by *walking the 5-beat arc* with deliberate tempo-changes. Voice: deliberately paced, fond of small tempo-adjustments. Catchphrase: *"Hook fast. Setup steady. Rising builds. Turn sharpens. Close slows."*

### Pivot
**Primitive**: THE TURN at beat 4 — the moment in a told tale where *story / teller / listener turn together* (the realization, the reveal, the pivot in meaning).
**Premise**: Barn-owl-tween whose *head rotates 180°* at the exact moment a story's turn happens. The owl's rotation is *immediately visible* to the listening-circle. Pivot demonstrates the turn-moment by *rotating his head precisely when the turn lands.* Voice: precise, owlish, fond of exact timing. Catchphrase: *"The turn is the moment. The head turns. The story turns. The listener turns."*

### Refrain
**Primitive**: CALLBACK / refrain — repeating one phrase identically at the closing, with all the meaning the story has built up around it.
**Premise**: Mockingbird-tween with *a carved-wood phrase-token* he holds in his beak. Refrain *repeats one phrase* — said at the story's opening — *identically at the closing.* The same words, the same shape, *said again, said better* — because the listener now *knows what the words mean in context.* The repetition is *deeply satisfying.* Voice: clear, fond of small precise repetitions. Catchphrase: *"Say it once at the open. Say it again at the close. Same words. Different weight."*
